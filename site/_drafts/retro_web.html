I'll begin with one simple statement: the yesterweb/"web 1.0" movement is, in my opinion, misguided and blinded by nostalgia.

If you're reading this you probably use Neocities (which currently hosts this website) or are at least aware it exists. If not, Neocities is a free personal web hosting service. The site aims to provide a platform for free self-expression and creativity, the supposed antithesis of the modern web. The prospect of a space online that's entirely yours is understandably very attractive to many people, and as such Neocities attracts users in droves.

Oftentimes on other platforms I'll see a post soapboxing and basically urging readers to join Neocities immediately. Maybe they'll even link to some helpful developer resources like sadgrl.online's webmastery resources or W3Schools. I really do hate to make assumptions (much less incorrect assumptions) but I can't help but assume that these posts are more often than not written by people in the "honeymoon" phase of their Neocities journey.

To be honest, it does sadden me a little to see a site on Neocities that clearly had a lot of effort and care put into it but hasn't been updated in a year or possibly longer. The webmaster most likely retreated to social media. But, I do wonder sometimes: if your website really meant that much to you, why did you abandon it?

I don't mean to be unrealistic. I know that very often life gets in the way and prevents people from tending to personal projects (my site updates relatively frequently due to a lack of the aforementioned). But it is quite frequently that I spot someone who manages to update frequently on social media and yet their website on Neocities is stale and failing to meet recent OSHA guidelines.
